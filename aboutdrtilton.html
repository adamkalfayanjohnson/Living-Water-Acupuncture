Margaret C. Tilton MD, MS


Dr. Tilton graduated from Tufts University with a B.S. in Biology, received her M.S. degree in Nutrition and Public Health from Columbia University and her M.D. degree from University of Medicine & Dentistry of New Jersey (now Rutgers Medical School). Her internship in Internal Medicine was at Hahnemann University Hospital in Philadelphia and her residency in Physical Medicine and Rehabilitation was completed at UMDNJ/Kessler Institute in New Jersey. Dr. Tilton completed her acupuncture training at the UCLA School of Medicine's Medical Acupuncture for Physicians program in 2001.

Dr. Tilton has over 25 years of experience in physical medicine and rehabilitation, during which she has incorporated acupuncture as an integral part of her clinical practice.

After practicing in academic medicine in New Jersey and independent group practice in New Hampshire, her new Cape Cod practice focuses exclusively on medical acupuncture. Dr. Tilton practices at the Healing Arts Collective in Orleans. Available by appointment only.

As an Outreach Ministry of the First Congregational Church in Wellfleet, Massachusetts, Dr. Tilton offers a free clinic for addiction/mental wellness treatment using an approach developed by the National Acupuncture Detoxification Association. This approach involves gentle placement of up to 5 small disposable needles into specific sites on each ear. The person being treated sits quietly in a group setting for 30-45 minutes. This service is available on Tuesdays from 3:00 PM-6:00 PM.
